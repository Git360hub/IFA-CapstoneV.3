Frequently Asked Questions

Mead is an alcoholic beverage made by fermenting honey mixed with water, often with the addition of fruits, spices, grains, or hops. The defining characteristic of mead is that the majority of its fermentable sugar comes from honey. Alcohol content in mead typically ranges from about 3.5% to over 20% by volume. Mead can be still, carbonated, or naturally sparkling, and its flavor profile varies widely-it can be dry, semi-sweet, or sweet, depending on the recipe and fermentation process.

There are many varieties of mead, including:

  • Show mead: Made with just honey, water, and yeast.
  • Melomel: Mead with added fruit.
  • Metheglin: Mead with added spices or herbs.
  • Braggot: Mead made with both honey and malted barley.
  • Cyser: Mead made with honey and apple juice or cider.
